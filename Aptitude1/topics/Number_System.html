1. The difference between the squares of two consecutive odd integers is always divisible by:


a. 3


b. 7


c. 6


d. 8

Let the two consecutive odd integers be (2x + 1) and (2x + 3) Then, (2x + 3)2 - (2x + 1)2 = (2x + 3 + 2x + 1) (2x + 3 - 2x - 1) = (4x + 4)(2) = 8 (x + 1), which is always divisible by 8
